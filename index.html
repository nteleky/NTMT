Hey TMT!

In a couple of hours, you’re going to be asked to sit in a room and vote for positions again. When you are, I want you to take a look around and see what I’ve done when I run to be your co-president again. This week, there has been a lot of campaigning. But I haven’t been campaigning with bumper stickers or buttons or yard signs and I didn’t start this week. I’ve been campaigning for the last seven months as your co-president.

When I made this website last year, I meant for it to be more than just a list of ideas that I wanted to turn into goals. We make a lot of promises at elections. I made this site so you could hold me to the promises I made when you elected me. I’m linking it now so you can see all the things I said I would accomplish this time last year. I put it up so you could see that everything I said I would do, I’ve done.

In the last seven months, I’ve become familiar with how things work — what’s reasonable, what’s achievable, and what’s practical for us as mockers and as students. Moving forward, I’m going to set goals again. Below, you can see my vision for next year. You’re not going to see ideas — you’re going to see plans. Because what I’ve learned this year is that plans are better than ideas. Plans are real. Plans are what can improve this club. Plans are what can help our program grow, help our people succeed, help us make next year our best ever. Plans matter. And I hope you’ll let me show you mine.
